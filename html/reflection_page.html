Reflection on the STM32-BALL-BALANCE-GAME Project

This reflection provides insights into the challenges, learning experiences, and potential improvements for the STM32-Ball-Balance-Game project.

Struggles and Obstacles

The project presented a myriad of technical challenges that required a deep understanding of embedded systems.

  • Understanding Interrupt Mechanisms: The intricacies of triggering interrupts and handling the Data Ready bit in the gyroscope proved to be a formidable challenge. Deciphering these mechanisms necessitated a thorough comprehension of the gyroscope’s workings and its interaction with the microcontroller.
  • Clock Configurations for RNG and LCD Drivers: Configuring clock settings for the RNG and LCD drivers was a complex task. It demanded a nuanced understanding of the peripheral clocking system, particularly its frequency and type. The process involved delving into the lower-level aspects of the system to ensure seamless integration and functionality.

What I Learned

The project was a profound learning journey, underscoring the importance of a holistic understanding of embedded systems.

  • Holistic System Understanding: A key takeaway was the criticality of grasping the system as a whole. Effective debugging often hinged on building a foundational knowledge of how various lines, ports, pins, and registers were configured. While treating functions as black boxes can expedite development, it sometimes obfuscates underlying issues that only a comprehensive understanding can unveil.

Fresh Restart: A Hypothetical Approach

Reflecting on the project, if given an opportunity to start afresh, I would:

  • Forego the HAL (Hardware Abstraction Layer): Opting to develop drivers from scratch, without relying on the HAL, would provide a richer learning experience. This approach would deepen my understanding of the microcontroller’s hardware and its low-level interactions.

Suggestions for Improvement

While the project achieved its goals, there are areas where enhancements could be made:

  • Addressing Degaussing in Rapid Movements: A persistent issue was the degaussing effect observed during rapid movements of the ball. While attempts were made to mitigate this by devising algorithms for dynamic sector-based clearing and redrawing on the LCD, this aspect requires further exploration and optimization.

Closing Thoughts

This project was not only a technical endeavor but also a journey of discovery and learning, highlighting the nuances and challenges of embedded systems development.
